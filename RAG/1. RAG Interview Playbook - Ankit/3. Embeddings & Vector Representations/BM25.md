# BM25 (Best Matching 25)

BM25 (Best Matching 25) is a state-of-the-art ranking algorithm in information retrieval that determines document relevance based on keyword frequency, acting as an improved successor to TF-IDF. It is generally better than TF-IDF because it prevents long documents from unfairly dominating results (length normalization) and limits the impact of repeatedly mentioned terms (term saturation). 

---

## What is BM25 Search?

BM25 is a probabilistic, "bag-of-words" retrieval function used by search engines (like Elasticsearch and Lucene) to rank documents based on their relevance to a user query. It uses two key concepts: 

- Term Frequency (TF): How often a query term appears in a document.
- Inverse Document Frequency (IDF): How rare a term is across the entire collection of documents. 

---

## How is BM25 Different from TF-IDF?

While both rely on the same fundamentals, BM25 improves on TF-IDF in two main ways: 

### Term Frequency Saturation
- In TF-IDF, if a word appears 100 times instead of 10, the score might be 10 times higher.
- In BM25, after a certain point, more occurrences provide diminishing returns, preventing overemphasis on keyword stuffing.

### Document Length Normalization
- TF-IDF often rewards long documents simply because they contain more words.
- BM25 penalizes or adjusts for document length, ensuring a short document with highly relevant terms ranks higher than a long document that just happens to mention the term multiple times.

---

## What is Better: BM25 vs. TF-IDF?

BM25 is generally better than TF-IDF. As discussed in this KMW Technology article, it is considered an improvement on TF-IDF. It offers better precision in most applications. 

- BM25: Ideal for modern search engines, providing more intuitive matches, specifically when dealing with documents of varying lengths.
- TF-IDF: Considered "old school" or traditional, suitable for simpler scenarios or when a baseline model is sufficient.
